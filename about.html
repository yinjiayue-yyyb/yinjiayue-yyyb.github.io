Journey of growth

My scientific journey began with a fascination for marine life and a desire to understand how living systems work. From Ningbo on the East China Sea to Guangzhou near the South China Sea, my academic path has unfolded across coastal regions rich in marine biodiversity.

At Ningbo University (NBU), my interest was first sparked by the unusual phenomenon of pelvic fin loss in silver pomfret, which introduced me to molecular experimentation. Later, at the South China Sea Institute of Oceanology (SCSIO), my doctoral training focused on shrimp health and pathogen resistance, where I learned to use transcriptomics and functional assays to uncover biological mechanisms.

Over time, this work led me to a broader question: how do organisms detect external cues and respond so precisely? That question now drives my growing interest in marine sensory biology and cell physiology.
